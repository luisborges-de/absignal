DATABANK ISSUER, LLC AND DATABANK CO-ISSUER, LLC
Data Center Issuance Notes, Series 2024-1 (DB 2024-1)

Sourced from public KBRA preliminary-ratings release for the DataBank Series 2024-1
transaction. Collateral facts below are as disclosed; covenant thresholds reflect the
standard data center ABS structure described by KBRA for this asset class.

TRANSACTION SUMMARY
Sponsor / Trust Manager: DataBank Holdings Ltd. This is the fourth series issued from the
DataBank Issuer / Co-Issuer master trust sharing a common collateral pool. KBRA assigned
preliminary ratings to the Series 2024-1 Class A-2 notes. Total issuance of approximately
$725,000,000. Anticipated Repayment Date in 2029. Closing date 2024.

COLLATERAL POOL
The collateral comprises 35 data centers totaling approximately 1.5 million square feet
and approximately 243 megawatts (MW) of critical load power. The pool is geographically
diverse with primary concentration in Dallas and Houston, Texas, and additional facilities
in Salt Lake City, Denver, Kansas City, Atlanta, Chicago, Minneapolis, Indianapolis,
Pittsburgh, Irvine, Las Vegas, Cleveland, Los Angeles and San Diego. 18 fee-simple sites
represent 62.3% of NOI and 15 leasehold sites represent 32.7% of NOI.

CASH FLOW WATERFALL AND PERFORMANCE TRIGGERS
Section 1 — Cash Trap. A Cash Trap Event occurs if the three-month average debt service
coverage ratio (DSCR) is less than 1.35x, diverting all excess cash flow to the Cash Trap
Reserve Account until cured.

Section 2 — Early Amortization. An Early Amortization Event occurs if the three-month
average DSCR falls below 1.15x.

Section 3 — LTV Sweep. If the loan-to-value ratio exceeds 70.0%, excess funds are applied
to mandatory deleveraging.

Section 4 — Tenant Concentration. If contracted revenue from any single tenant exceeds
40.0% of total portfolio revenue, an Enhanced Reserve Event occurs.

Section 5 — Senior Interest Reserve. The issuer maintains a senior interest reserve
account balance of at least $12,000,000.

Section 6 — Anticipated Repayment Date. Failure to repay by the Anticipated Repayment Date
triggers hyper-amortization and an interest rate step-up.
