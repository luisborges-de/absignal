CENTERSQUARE ISSUER LLC AND CENTERSQUARE CO-ISSUER LLC
Secured Data Center Revenue Notes, Series 2025-1 and Series 2025-2

Sourced from public KBRA preliminary-ratings release and Asset Securitization Report
coverage of the transaction. Figures below are as disclosed publicly.

TRANSACTION SUMMARY
Sponsor / Manager: Phoenix Infrastructure (formed from Cyxtera Technologies and Evoque
Data Center Solutions). KBRA assigned preliminary ratings to three classes of notes.
Series 2025-1 Class A and Class B notes are rated A- (sf) and BBB- (sf); Series 2025-2
Class A notes are rated A- (sf). Total issuance of approximately $985,000,000.
Series 2025-1 has an Anticipated Repayment Date in March 2030. Series 2025-2 has an
Anticipated Repayment Date in March 2032. Closing date March 2025.

COLLATERAL POOL (as of December 31, 2024)
The collateral comprises 26 multi-customer enterprise data centers (10 fee-simple and
16 leasehold), representing approximately 150.0 megawatts (MW) of power capacity and
approximately 1,168,428 square feet across multiple U.S. markets, concentrated in the
Northern Virginia / Mid-Atlantic region. Total Annualized Monthly Recurring Revenue
(AMRR) is $438.8 million and Total Annualized Adjusted Net Operating Income (AANOI) is
$208.9 million. The pool serves 1,081 unique customers across 15 industries. The largest
single tenant represents 8.0% of AMRR and the top 20 tenants represent approximately
39.7% of AMRR. The weighted average remaining contract term is 3.9 years. Class A
loan-to-value is 59% and Class B loan-to-value is 63%.

CASH FLOW WATERFALL AND PERFORMANCE TRIGGERS
Section 1 — Cash Trap. If the three-month average debt service coverage ratio (DSCR) is
less than 1.35x, then all excess cash will be deposited in the Cash Trap Reserve Account
until the condition is cured.

Section 2 — Tax / Expense Reserve. If the three-month average DSCR is less than 1.75x
(before amortization), then collections will be used to reserve twelve months of real
estate and property taxes.

Section 3 — LTV Test. The Class A loan-to-value ratio is 59% at issuance. A Class A LTV
Test Event occurs and excess funds are applied to mandatory deleveraging if the Class A
loan-to-value ratio exceeds 67.0%.

Section 4 — Anticipated Repayment Date. If the notes are not repaid by their Anticipated
Repayment Date, the notes begin hyper-amortization and the interest rate steps up.
