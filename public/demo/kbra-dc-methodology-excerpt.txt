Adapted from KBRA Rating Methodology - January 2026

3.14(b) Cash Trap Test. If the three-month rolling average DSCR falls below 1.35x (the
"Cash Trap Threshold"), a Cash Trap Event shall be deemed to have occurred. All Available
Funds distributable to the Class B Notes and Residual Interest shall be redirected to fund
the Senior Principal Reserve Account until the threshold is no longer breached for three
consecutive Determination Dates. Section reference: Section 3.14(b)(ii).

3.14(c) Early Amortisation Test. If the three-month rolling average DSCR falls below 1.10x
(the "Early Amortisation Threshold"), an Early Amortisation Event shall occur and the
transaction shall enter accelerated repayment of all Note classes in priority order.
Section reference: Section 3.14(c)(i).

3.15 Loan-to-Value Sweep Test. If the LTV Ratio exceeds 65.0%, the issuer shall apply all
Available Funds in excess of scheduled interest to mandatory deleveraging of Class A Senior
Notes until the LTV Ratio returns below 65.0%. Section reference: Section 3.15(a).

3.16 Occupancy Reserve Trigger. If weighted average occupancy falls below 75.0%, the
issuer shall maintain an Enhanced Liquidity Reserve equal to six months of projected
Class A interest payments. Section reference: Section 3.16(b).

3.17 Tenant Concentration Covenant. Contracted revenue from any single tenant shall not
exceed 40.0% of total portfolio revenue. A breach requires an enhanced 12-month senior
interest reserve. Section reference: Section 3.17(a).

3.18 WART Covenant. Portfolio weighted average remaining lease term shall be maintained
above 2.5 years. Breach triggers an Enhanced Reserve. Section reference: Section 3.18.

3.19 Expense Reserve Trigger. The issuer shall maintain an Operating Expense Reserve of
at least $6,000,000 at all times. Section reference: Section 3.19.

3.20 Additional Issuance Tests. Additional Notes may only be issued if DSCR (pro forma)
>= 1.35x, LTV (pro forma) <= 65.0%, and occupancy >= 75.0%. Section reference: Section 3.20.
